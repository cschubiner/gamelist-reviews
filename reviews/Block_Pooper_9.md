## Gameplay
**What Critics/Players Say:**
- Physics-based action where players control flying heads that move "like rockets"
- Core mechanic involves collecting falling points while preventing a heart from being consumed
- Unique defensive mechanic: players excrete blocks onto opponents below them
- Fast-paced, chaotic party game design
- Described officially as "ride a high velocity stream of blocky effluence to glory"

---

## Mechanics
**What Critics/Players Say:**
- Local multiplayer-focused design
- Physics-driven movement system
- Competitive point collection combined with cooperative objectives
- Controller support for Xbox, PS4, Switch Pro, Wiimotes, and keyboard
- Some technical issues reported with wireless Xbox One controllers (working in menus but not during gameplay)
- Questions raised about PC key binding clarity

## Fun Factor
**What Critics/Players Say:**
- **Overall Steam Rating:** Mixed reception - 67% positive (19 positive / 9 negative out of 28 reviews)
- Community enthusiasm evident in discussions about pushing player limits
- Developer humor and engagement with community suggests lighthearted tone
- Free-to-play model makes it accessible for party gaming
- Humorous premise appeals to players looking for quirky multiplayer experiences

## Player Count Experience
**What Critics/Players Say:**
- **Originally supported:** At least 6 local players
- **Expanded capacity:** Developer announced successful testing with **16 simultaneous controllers** (January 2019 update)
- Developer enthusiastically stated: "NOTHING IN LIFE WILL MAKE YOU HAPPIER THAN 16 PLAYER BLOCKPOOPER"
- Community tracking player records for maximum local player counts
- Strong emphasis on local multiplayer as the core experience
- Supports Shared/Split Screen PvP and Remote Play Together features

**What Critics/Players Say:**

**Critical Reception:**
