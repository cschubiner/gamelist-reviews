# Dominions - Priests, Prophets & Pretenders

## Overview
Dominions is a 4X turn-based strategy game where players take control of a powerful being that rules a nation and aspires to godhood in a fantasy world filled with monsters, magic items, and world-altering spells. Each pretender god is Lord and Master of one of fourteen cultures, each with its own individual strengths and weaknesses.

## Gameplay & Mechanics

### Scale and Content
- 2 to 14 "pretender gods" battle for global supremacy
- Over 600 units
- 400 magic spells
- 300 magical items
- Hundreds of provinces on each map

### Combat and Territory
- Main map divided into provinces
- Each battle takes place within a province for control
- Hundreds of provinces possible on a single map
- Tactical combat system

### Dominion Mechanics
- Nations are weaker while fighting in enemy dominion
- Nations are stronger while fighting in their own dominion
- Dominion spreads through multiple methods:
  - Temples (one per province)
  - Priests preaching
  - Prophets spreading influence

### Priests and Prophets
- **Prophets**: Only one per nation at a time
- **Priests**: Unlimited number possible
- Both have holy magic and preaching abilities
- Mobile temple functionality
- Can cast holy spells effective against certain enemies

### Magic System
- Eight paths (elements) of magic
- Mages must research spells before casting
- Seven branches of magic to research
- Progressively more powerful spells unlocked through research
- Spell types include:
  - Summoning powerful demons
  - Offensive blast spells
  - Support and utility magic
  - Artifact forging capabilities

### Pretender Customization
- Create gods with varying focuses
- Options from magic-focused but physically weak
- To near-unkillable warrior-gods with minimal magic
- Strategic pretender design affects entire game

## Fun Factor
Dominions offers incredibly deep strategic gameplay with almost overwhelming options and complexity. The game rewards careful planning, understanding of game systems, and long-term strategic thinking. Best suited for players who enjoy deep, complex strategy games and don't mind a steep learning curve. The variety of nations, spells, and strategies provides nearly endless replayability.

## Player Count Experience
- **Single Player**: Full campaign against AI
- **Multiplayer**: 2-14 players
- **Hot Seat**: Pass-and-play multiplayer
- **Play-by-Email**: Turn-based multiplayer over time

## Overall Reception

### Critical Reception
Dominions has developed a cult following among hardcore strategy game fans. While not mainstream, it's highly respected in turn-based strategy circles.

### Player Feedback
**Strengths:**
- Incredible depth and complexity
- Massive variety of strategies
- Unique dominion and magic systems
- High replayability
- Rich fantasy setting

**Challenges:**
- Very steep learning curve
- Complex systems require significant time investment
- Dated graphics and interface
- Not beginner-friendly

### Legacy
Dominions is the first game in what became a successful series, with Dominions 2-6 following. The original established the core mechanics that would be refined in later entries.

### Scores
- Niche but highly regarded among strategy enthusiasts
- No mainstream review scores available
- Strong dedicated player base

## Notes
This is the original Dominions game that started the series. Players new to the franchise might want to consider later entries (Dominions 5 or 6) which feature improved graphics and refined gameplay systems while maintaining the core mechanics that make the series unique.
