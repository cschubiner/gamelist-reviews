# Scribble Plus Review

## Overview
Scribble Plus (also known as Scribble+) is a family-friendly drawing and guessing party game designed for players of all ages. It distinguishes itself from competitors like Pictionary by being specifically designed to include children alongside adults through specialized difficulty modes and accessible gameplay.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
Scribble Plus is a quick-draw multiplayer party game where players:
- Draw pictures based on word prompts
- Guess what others are drawing
- Compete for points and laughs

The game features:
- **Over 1,500 words** from 17 categories (Objects, Places, People, and more)
- **Easy Mode:** Specifically designed for children to play alongside adults
- **"Difficult" category:** For experienced players
- **Multiple game modes:** Different ways to play for variety
- **Just Doodle mode:** No timer or scoring - perfect for practice or creative freedom
- **Mutators:** Gameplay modifiers for additional variety

Players connect via web browsers on phones, tablets, laptops, or desktops - no app download required. The game displays on a TV, laptop, or tablet as the central screen.

## Fun Factor
Reviews highlight Scribble Plus as "tons of fun and flexibility" with "simple yet fun" gameplay. The family-friendly design means parents can actually play with children in a balanced way, unlike many party games that either bore adults or frustrate kids.

One review noted: "Rarely does a family like mine want to play a game together that isn't labelled 'Mario' on the front," suggesting it successfully bridges generational gaps. The Just Doodle mode is particularly clever, allowing creative freedom without competitive pressure.

## Verdict
Scribble Plus successfully fills a niche for genuinely family-friendly party gaming. While drawing and guessing games are common, the specific design choices - Easy mode for children, Just Doodle mode for casual play, and thoughtful category selection - make it stand out as a game that truly works across generations.

The critical scores in the 78-80 range indicate solid quality, and reviewer comments emphasize its unique ability to engage entire families. The 1,500+ word bank is substantial, and the variety in game modes suggests good replay value. The browser-based controller system eliminates technical barriers.

If you're specifically looking for party games that include children without dumbing down the experience for adults, Scribble Plus appears to be an excellent choice. For adult-only groups, Jackbox's Drawful might offer more edge, but for mixed-age gatherings, Scribble Plus seems purpose-built for the task. The positive critical reception and emphasis on returning to play it again suggest strong entertainment value.
