# Takelings House Party

## Overview
**Platform:** PC (Steam), VR (Meta Quest, Oculus Rift), iOS
**Players:** 1 VR player + up to 8 smartphone players
**Price:** Available on Steam (20% discount often available)
**Release Date:** Early Access
**Developer/Publisher:** Takelings

## Gameplay & Mechanics

Takelings House Party is a frantic and whimsical asymmetric local multiplayer party game that pits one VR player against up to 8 smartphone-wielding couch players in a variety of chaotic minigames. The VR player navigates a virtual environment while smartphone players (who connect via WiFi with no app download required) attempt to hide, sneak, attack, and cause mayhem in various creative ways.

The game features five unique levels, each designed around the asymmetric gameplay concept where the VR player has different objectives and abilities compared to the smartphone players. The gameplay emphasizes teamwork among the phone players while the VR player attempts to complete objectives or defend against the chaos.

## Fun Factor

Players describe Takelings House Party as highly entertaining and engaging, particularly praising its unique party mechanics that make it a hit at gatherings. The asymmetric gameplay creates hilarious moments as smartphone players coordinate to challenge the VR player. Reviews indicate the game is fun enough for 2-3 hour play sessions with family or friends.

However, some players note that with only five levels, content can become repetitive after extended play. The novelty of the asymmetric VR vs. smartphone gameplay remains the primary draw.

## Player Count Experience

**Optimal:** 1 VR player + 4-8 smartphone players
**Supported:** 1 VR player + 1-8 smartphone players

The game truly shines with larger groups, as the chaos and coordination among smartphone players creates more dynamic and unpredictable gameplay. Solo or small group play is possible but doesn't capture the full party game experience the title aims to deliver.

## Technical Considerations

The most significant technical concern reported by players involves Steam Remote Play functionality. When it works properly, the experience is highly enjoyable, but bugs and connectivity problems can occasionally hamper gameplay. As an Early Access title, the game continues to receive development updates to address these issues.

## Overall Reception

**Steam User Score:** 79% Positive (298 reviews)
**Critical Reception:** Limited professional reviews, but community feedback is largely positive
**Status:** Early Access with ongoing development

### Strengths:
- Innovative asymmetric VR + smartphone gameplay
- Highly entertaining for groups and parties
- Easy accessibility (smartphones as controllers with WiFi connection)
- Engaging multiplayer mechanics

### Weaknesses:
- Limited content (5 levels) can feel repetitive
- Technical issues with connectivity and Steam Remote Play
- Still in Early Access with ongoing development needs

## Verdict

Takelings House Party succeeds in creating a unique and entertaining asymmetric party experience that leverages VR technology while making it accessible to non-VR players through smartphone integration. While technical issues and limited content present challenges, the core gameplay concept is strong and particularly well-suited for social gatherings. Best enjoyed with larger groups who can embrace the chaotic, competitive nature of the minigames.

**Recommended for:** VR owners looking for party games, groups seeking unique multiplayer experiences, fans of asymmetric gameplay
