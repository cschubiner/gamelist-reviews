# JoustMania Review Summary

---

## Overview
JoustMania is a free-to-play, movement-based party game inspired by Johann Sebastian Joust. It's a screen-free game where players hold PlayStation Move controllers and try to keep their own controller still while jostling opponents' controllers. The game is available on Windows (Steam) and Raspberry Pi.

---

## Gameplay & Mechanics

**Core Mechanic:**
- Screen-free gameplay centered on the "jostling" mechanic
- Players must keep their PlayStation Move controllers steady while disrupting others
- Music speed determines sensitivity levels (slow music = high sensitivity, fast music = more lenient movement)
- Five adjustable sensitivity levels (Ultra High to Ultra Low)

**Game Modes:**
- 13 different game modes including:
  - Joust Free-for-All
  - Teams variants
  - Traitors
  - Werewolves
  - Zombies
  - Commander
  - Swapper
  - Fight Club
  - Tournament
  - Non-Stop Joust
  - Ninja Bomb

**Technical Features:**
- Browser-based UI interface for settings (127.0.0.1)
- Admin mode for customizing settings during gameplay
- Remote Play Together capability
- Shared/split-screen PvP and co-op support

---

## Player Count Experience

**Capacity:**
- Windows: Up to 7 players
- Raspberry Pi: 18+ players
- Optimized for conventions and large group gatherings

**Session Duration:**
- Rounds last 1-5 minutes
- Designed for quick, repeatable gameplay
- Convention mode allows continuous play with rotating players

**Age Accessibility:**
- ESRB Rating: Everyone 10+
- PEGI Rating: 7 (for violence)
- Recommended skill level: 5+ years old
- Suitable for mixed-age groups with supervision

---

## Fun Factor

**Positive Aspects:**
- Described as "a fun real life game" and "the perfect group activity"
- Emphasizes physical activity - "Exercise without realizing it"
- More of a physical activity/family game than traditional video game
- Simple, communication-based fun suitable for parties
- Automatic gameplay announcements help new players learn quickly
- Designed for easy setup at conventions and self-running with large groups

**Physical & Social Elements:**
- Encourages real-world movement and interaction
- Supports competitive and cooperative play styles
- Team play, vulnerable king modes, and traitor mechanics add variety
- No screen required makes it unique and accessible

---

## Overall Reception

**User Reviews:**
- Steam: 92% positive rating (from 13 user reviews)
- 6 Steam curators have reviewed the product
- GitHub repository: 138 stars and 39 forks (indicates community interest)
- Project views: 2,708 on Hackster.io

**Community Engagement:**
- Active contributor base (CONTRIBUTORS.md file maintained)
- Used successfully at conventions
- Cross-platform support (Windows, Mac, Raspberry Pi)

**Technical Challenges Noted:**
- Some Windows compatibility issues reported
- CPU usage problems (100% in some cases)
- PS Move controller connectivity issues after Windows 10 updates
- Admin interface bugs reported
- Requires PlayStation Move controllers and Bluetooth setup

---

## Key Strengths & Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Unique screen-free gameplay
- Excellent for parties and large groups
- Free to play
- 13 diverse game modes
- Scales well (up to 18+ players on Raspberry Pi)
- Family-friendly
- Active development and community support

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited formal critical reviews from major outlets
- Small user review base on Steam (only 13 reviews)
- Requires specific hardware (PlayStation Move controllers)
- Technical issues on Windows reported
- Not accessible without proper controllers and setup
