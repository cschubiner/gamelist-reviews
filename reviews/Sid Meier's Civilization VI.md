# Sid Meier's Civilization VI

## Overview
Sid Meier's Civilization VI is the sixth main installment in the legendary Civilization series. Released in 2016, it builds upon the foundation of Civilization V while introducing new systems like district placement, active research, and enhanced diplomacy. The game aims to be the best 4X strategy game yet made, offering unprecedented depth and strategic options.

## Gameplay & Mechanics

### Core 4X Gameplay
- **eXplore**: Discover continents, natural wonders, and other civilizations
- **eXpand**: Found and develop cities across the map
- **eXploit**: Manage resources, trade routes, and empire development
- **eXterminate**: Engage in warfare, diplomacy, or cultural dominance

### Revolutionary District System
- Cities now spread across multiple tiles
- Specialized districts for different focuses (Science, Culture, Military, etc.)
- District placement affects yields and bonuses
- Strategic city planning becomes crucial
- Wonders also require their own tiles

### Active Research
- "Eureka" moments boost research progress
- "Inspiration" moments for civic development
- Historical actions accelerate corresponding research
- More dynamic technology progression

### Enhanced Diplomacy
- World Congress system
- Emergency situations requiring global response
- Alliance system with different types and levels
- More nuanced diplomatic relationships

### Victory Conditions
- Domination (military conquest)
- Science (Mars colonization)
- Cultural (tourism dominance)
- Religious (religious conversion)
- Diplomatic (diplomatic favor)
- Score (time-based)

## Fun Factor
Civilization VI offers the series' deepest strategic experience with the district system adding layers of city planning strategy. The "one more turn" addictiveness remains strong, and the game provides even more ways to customize your civilization's development. The expansions (Rise and Fall, Gathering Storm) significantly enhance the experience with new mechanics and systems. Perfect for strategy enthusiasts who want maximum depth and replayability.

## Player Count Experience
- **Single Player**: Extensive campaign against sophisticated AI
- **Multiplayer**: Online and LAN support
  - Simultaneous turns
  - Various game speed options
  - Hot seat for local play
  - Up to 12 civilizations per game (base game)
  - Expanded player counts with DLC

## Overall Reception

### Critical Reception
Critics have praised Civilization VI as a pinnacle of the 4X strategy genre:

**Positive Reviews:**
- "Sid Meier's Civilization VI is a joy to play, and the best the series has produced"
- "The best 4X strategy game yet made"
- Praised for strategic depth and options
- District system widely acclaimed
- Active research system adds dynamism

**Expansions Enhanced Experience:**
- Rise and Fall added loyalty and golden/dark ages
- Gathering Storm (Metascore: 80) added climate change and natural disasters
- Expansions considered essential for full experience

### Player Feedback

**Strengths:**
- District system adds strategic city planning
- Most content-rich Civilization at launch
- Beautiful art style and presentation
- Enhanced diplomatic options
- Strong replayability with many civilizations
- Active modding community

**Debates:**
- Some players prefer Civilization V's streamlined approach
- Art style divisive (some prefer V's more realistic look)
- AI diplomacy still has quirks
- Learning curve steeper than previous entries

### Scores
- **Metacritic**: Positive critical reception across platforms
- **Gathering Storm Expansion**: 80 (Generally Favorable)
- **Steam**: Very Positive overall
- **Critical Consensus**: Best Civilization game for depth and options

### Community
- Very active player base
- Extensive modding community
- Regular updates and balance patches
- Strong competitive multiplayer scene
- Active content creation and streaming

## Notes
- Expansions highly recommended for full experience
- Gathering Storm expansion adds critical late-game systems
- System requirements higher than Civilization V
- Regular updates and new civilization DLC
- Cross-platform play available for some versions
- Epic Games Store exclusive period ended, now on Steam

## Comparison to Civilization V
- More complex and deeper systems
- Steeper learning curve
- District system vs. simpler city layout
- Some prefer V's refinement, others prefer VI's depth
- Both excellent games with different strengths
