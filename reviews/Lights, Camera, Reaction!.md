# Lights, Camera, Reaction!

## Overview
**Platform:** PC (Steam), VR
**Players:** Single-player, 2-6 local multiplayer, 3-8 online smartphone multiplayer
**Price:** Available on Steam
**Release Date:** Released with ongoing updates
**Developer/Publisher:** Fluxean

## Gameplay & Mechanics

Lights, Camera, Reaction! is described as "the ultimate party game for family gatherings and get-togethers," offering multiple play modes across VR and non-VR options with over 50+ mini-games focused on quick reactions.

**Single Player Mode:**
Features over 50+ progressively faster mini-games where players test their reaction times. The mode includes:
- Power-ups to slow down time
- Boss fights for variety
- Diverse reaction challenges: mowing down zombies, sniping targets, chopping off obstacles (including the humorous "man buns"), and playing retro 2D platformers
- Progressive difficulty that accelerates as players succeed

**Local Multiplayer (2-6 Players):**
Players take turns wearing the VR headset to compete in 6 unique game modes. The format involves VR players either collaborating or competing with local players, creating asymmetric gameplay dynamics.

**Online Smartphone Multiplayer (3-8 Players):**
The party leader plays from the VR headset while 3-8 players connect via smartphones to participate. This allows for remote party gaming where friends can join from different locations using their mobile devices.

**Game Variety:**
The 50+ mini-games ensure substantial variety, covering action scenarios (zombie combat, sniping), physical challenges (chopping), and even retro gaming tributes (2D platformers), all unified by the reaction-time focus.

## Fun Factor

Lights, Camera, Reaction! emphasizes fast-paced, accessible fun through reaction-based mini-games. The variety of 50+ different challenges prevents monotony, while the progressive difficulty in single-player mode creates a "just one more try" engagement loop.

The inclusion of humorous elements (like "chopping off man buns") suggests a lighthearted tone appropriate for family and social gatherings. The multiple play modes (solo, local, online) provide flexibility for different social contexts - play alone for practice, locally for traditional party gaming, or online for remote friends.

However, limited review data makes it difficult to assess how well the mini-games are executed and whether the variety remains engaging over extended sessions.

## Verdict

Lights, Camera, Reaction! presents an ambitious package with 50+ mini-games and flexible play modes spanning single-player, local multiplayer, and online smartphone participation. The variety of gameplay types and reaction challenges suggests substantial content, while the multiple modes accommodate diverse social gaming scenarios.

However, the critical lack of reviews and community validation makes this a risky purchase. With no Metacritic user reviews and limited Steam feedback, potential buyers have minimal information about actual game quality, mini-game execution, or long-term entertainment value. This could indicate either a very recent release that hasn't gained traction, marketing challenges, or quality issues preventing positive word-of-mouth.

The concept and feature set are strong - reaction-based mini-games work well for parties, the online smartphone mode addresses modern remote gaming needs, and the solo content provides value between multiplayer sessions. But without community validation, it's impossible to confidently recommend the game.

**Recommendation Status:** Intriguing but unproven - proceed with caution

**Potentially Recommended for:** VR owners seeking party games, families wanting multi-generational gaming, remote friend groups, reaction game enthusiasts, those willing to gamble on unproven titles

**Not Recommended for:** Risk-averse buyers, those requiring community validation, players expecting polished AAA experiences, groups seeking established party game franchises

**Suggested Approach:** Wait for more reviews and community feedback before purchasing, or purchase during a sale if the concept strongly appeals and you're comfortable with the risk of an unproven title.
