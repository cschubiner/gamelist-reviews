# Constructionary

## Overview
**Platform:** PC (Steam), VR
**Players:** 2+ players (various modes support different player counts)
**Price:** $3.99 on Steam
**Release Date:** August 13, 2019
**Developer/Publisher:** Unknown

## Gameplay & Mechanics

Constructionary is a VR party game that modernizes the classic Charades formula by replacing miming with virtual construction. One player wearing a VR headset builds objects out of blocks using hand-tracking controllers while other players attempt to guess what's being constructed.

**Core Gameplay Loop:**
1. VR player receives a word or phrase prompt
2. They build a representation using virtual blocks and their hands
3. Other players watch and attempt to guess the object/concept
4. Points awarded for successful guesses and quick completion

**Content:**
The game features over 1,000 built-in words and phrases, carefully curated to work well with the block-building mechanic. This substantial library helps prevent repetition and maintains long-term replayability.

**Game Modes:**
- **Player vs. Player:** Competitive individual scoring
- **Team vs. Team:** Group competition with collaborative guessing
- **Co-op Mode:** Cooperative play where teams work together

**Special Features:**
- Hidden pieces add puzzle-solving complexity
- First-person camera option for different perspectives
- Guides and tutorials designed for VR newcomers
- Accessible to players with no VR experience

## Fun Factor

Constructionary successfully translates the Charades concept into VR, creating intuitive party game fun. The building mechanic is more forgiving than drawing (as in Pictionary) since 3D construction allows viewing from multiple angles, yet more expressive than traditional Charades miming.

The variety of game modes accommodates different group dynamics - competitive players can battle individually while casual groups can cooperate or form teams. The accessibility features and tutorials help onboard VR-inexperienced players, which is crucial for party games where not everyone may be familiar with VR technology.

However, extremely limited review data (only 2 reviews in some regions) makes it difficult to assess how the game performs in actual party settings or whether the 1,000+ word list maintains long-term interest.

## Player Count Experience

**Optimal:** 4-6 players (1 VR + 3-5 guessing)
**Supported:** 2+ players (1 VR minimum + at least 1 guesser)
**Flexibility:** Modes support different group sizes

Like most VR party games, Constructionary requires at least one VR headset with one player using it while others guess. The game scales to accommodate larger groups through team modes, though practical limits depend on viewing screen size and space for observers.

The need for a VR headset represents both a strength (immersive building experience) and limitation (requires hardware investment that many potential players lack). However, the game's accessibility features help maximize the potential player pool among those who do have VR access.

## Verdict

Constructionary presents a clever adaptation of Charades for VR with thoughtful features like extensive word libraries, multiple game modes, and accessibility for newcomers. The $3.99 price point makes it a low-risk purchase for VR owners seeking party content.

However, the extreme lack of reviews (only 2) and minimal community presence raise significant concerns. This could indicate either a very recent release that hasn't gained traction, poor marketing, or quality issues that prevented word-of-mouth growth. Without substantial user feedback, it's impossible to confidently assess whether the game delivers on its promise.

For VR owners seeking party games and willing to take a gamble at the low price point, Constructionary might provide entertainment. However, the lack of community validation means buyers should have modest expectations and be prepared for potential issues.
