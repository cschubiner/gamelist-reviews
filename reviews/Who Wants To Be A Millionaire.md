# Who Wants To Be A Millionaire

## Overview
The video game adaptation of the famous TV quiz show "Who Wants To Be A Millionaire" brings the excitement of the television program to gaming platforms. Players answer trivia questions to progress through increasingly difficult levels, using lifelines and competing for the virtual million-dollar prize.

## Gameplay & Mechanics

### Question System
- Over 6,000 questions to test general knowledge
- More than 15 diverse topics
- Progressive difficulty system
- Questions organized by value tiers

### Lifelines
- Classic lifelines from the TV show
- Strategic use required for difficult questions
- Familiar mechanics for fans of the show

### Game Modes
- Single-player campaign
- Multiplayer options
- Authentic TV show atmosphere
- Multiple game variants

## Fun Factor
The game captures the tension and excitement of the TV show format. The massive question database provides variety, though the experience can be diminished by issues with question quality and technical problems. Best enjoyed by trivia enthusiasts and fans of the original show.

## Overall Reception

### Critical Reception
Mixed reviews from critics and players, with significant concerns about quality control and content accuracy.

### Player Feedback

**Common Criticisms:**
- **Question Quality Issues**: Way too many poorly worded questions and factually incorrect answers
- **Factual Errors**: Examples include Zinedine Zidane listed as playing in 4 World Cups (actually 3), Muhammad Ali's pre-conversion name listed as "EVANDER HOLYFIELD"
- **Lack of Polish**: Game feels "super rushed and lazy"
- **Poor Voice Acting**: Terrible voice work noted by multiple reviewers
- **Technical Issues**: Video settings show in French while other settings show in English despite proper language configuration
- **Quality Control**: Very poor quality control overall

**Positive Aspects:**
- Good graphics that capture the show's atmosphere
- Large question database (6,000+ questions)
- Familiar lifeline mechanics
- Multiple topic coverage

### Scores
- **Steam Reviews**: Mixed - 52% positive (357 reviews)
- **Overall Sentiment**: Below average due to quality issues

## Notes
While the game has potential as a trivia game adaptation of the beloved TV show, it suffers from poor execution, factual errors, and quality control issues that significantly detract from the experience. Only recommended for hardcore fans willing to overlook these problems or when purchased at steep discounts.
