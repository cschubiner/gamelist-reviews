# SpaceBoots

## Overview
SpaceBoots is a multiplayer twin-stick action shooter set in zero gravity environments where players battle using various weapons while navigating with magnetic boots and thrusters. Available on Steam with an upcoming Xbox release, the game emphasizes spatial movement and combat in 2D zero-gravity arenas.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
SpaceBoots delivers twin-stick shooter action with unique zero-gravity movement mechanics that differentiate it from traditional arena shooters.

**Movement System:**
- **Magnetic Boots:** Each player's boots can attach to walls and other surfaces, providing stable firing positions
- **Thrusters:** Built-in thrusters allow trajectory adjustments and movement between surfaces
- **Zero Gravity Physics:** Players float and drift, requiring spatial awareness and momentum management

**Combat:**
- **Variety of Weapons:** Multiple weapon types for different tactical approaches
- **Arena Battles:** Players compete in contained 2D spaces with various surface configurations
- **Twin-Stick Controls:** Standard twin-stick shooter control scheme for movement and aiming

The combination of magnetic attachment, thruster movement, and zero-gravity physics creates a unique combat experience where positioning and movement planning are as important as aiming and shooting.

## Fun Factor
SpaceBoots offers a fresh take on arena shooters through its zero-gravity mechanics and magnetic boot system. The movement creates strategic depth - players must decide when to attach to surfaces for stable shooting versus floating freely for mobility.

The twin-stick shooter genre is well-established and generally delivers solid entertainment when executed competently. The zero-gravity twist adds novelty and requires players to think spatially about combat approaches and positioning.

The magnetic boots create interesting tactical choices: attached players gain stability but lose mobility, while floating players have movement freedom but less control. This risk-reward dynamic can create engaging moment-to-moment decisions.
