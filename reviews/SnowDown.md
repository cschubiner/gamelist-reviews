# SnowDown

## Gameplay & Mechanics

SnowDown is a shared-screen party game supporting up to 12 players who use their phones or web browsers as controllers. The game centers around physics-based snowball combat where players aim to destroy their opponents' forts and knock out enemy players.

The core mechanic involves pulling back on a virtual slingshot and releasing to send snowballs flying across the screen. Players must master aim and trajectory to land effective hits. Building up the TURBO meter through successful throws and hits provides enhanced abilities - landing hits on opponents and their fortifications grants maximum TURBO gain.

Strategic depth comes from identifying weak points in opponent structures. Finding and exploiting these vulnerabilities can bring entire forts crashing down with the right shot. The physics-based destruction creates dynamic, emergent gameplay where each match plays out differently.

Randomized power-ups appear throughout matches, adding variety and unpredictability to each round. These power-ups provide temporary advantages that can turn the tide of battle, ensuring maximum snow fort destruction and keeping matches exciting.

The game supports computer-controlled players with adjustable difficulties. These AI players can be added to either team to balance teams or create cooperative challenges, making the game functional even without a full complement of human players.

## Fun Factor

SnowDown captures the playful essence of snowball fights while adding strategic fort destruction elements. The physics-based throwing mechanics create satisfying moments when a well-aimed shot triggers cascading destruction of an opponent's fort. The slingshot control scheme is intuitive and easy to understand but offers enough depth for skill development.

The shared-screen format where everyone views the same battlefield combined with individual phone controls creates an engaging hybrid setup. Players can see the action unfold on the big screen while maintaining personal control through their devices. The no-app requirement (phone controls via web browser) removes setup friction.

The randomized power-ups keep matches fresh and unpredictable, preventing strategies from becoming stale. The TURBO meter system rewards active, aggressive play while providing comeback mechanics for teams that land clutch hits. The adjustable AI difficulty ensures the game remains challenging and engaging even with fewer human players.

## Player Count Experience

SnowDown supports 2-12 players using phones or web browsers as controllers. The 12-player maximum provides substantial capacity for parties and gatherings, though it falls short of some competitors supporting 16+ players.

The ability to add computer-controlled players to either team is a significant feature. This allows for balanced matches regardless of human player count and enables cooperative modes where teams of humans face challenging AI opponents together. The adjustable difficulty means the AI can scale from easy practice to genuine challenge.

The shared-screen format requires a display device (TV or PC screen) that everyone can see, making it ideal for in-person gatherings but limiting online play potential. The phone-as-controller system means any gathering of friends with smartphones can play without needing physical controllers.

The team-based structure encourages cooperation and coordination, making it suitable for competitive play between groups or collaborative challenges against AI.
