## Game Overview

**Fish Volleyball** is a physics-driven local multiplayer arcade volleyball game where players control fish characters. It supports up to 10 players (5v5 maximum) in shared-screen matches.

---

## GAMEPLAY

**What Critics/Users Say:**
- Described as "QWOP meets Slime Volleyball" - emphasizing quirky physics-based movement
- Single-button controls with joystick (described as "Easy to learn, hard to master")
- Supports 1v1 to 5v5 configurations with local multiplayer focus
- Players describe it as having a high skill ceiling despite simple controls

---

## MECHANICS

**What Critics/Users Say:**
- Physics-driven movement requiring precise timing, positioning, and control
- Features complete volleyball mechanics: spikes, blocks, rallies, sets, serves, pancakes, spins, and aces
- "Profoundly-complex mechanics" despite one-button control scheme
- Heavy emphasis on mastering the physics to succeed
- Multiple game modifiers for customization (creating "millions of possible custom matches")

---

## FUN FACTOR

**Positive Feedback:**
- "Very cool, for a large group of friends it's just a godsend" - emphasizing party game appeal
- Best suited for local multiplayer gatherings
- Described as "chaotic" when played with more players (5v5 "pandemonium")
- The flopping fish physics appear to be a source of humor and entertainment

**Concerns:**
- Some players find it "too hard to survive as a local multiplayer game"
- Difficulty curve may be steep for casual players

## PLAYER COUNT EXPERIENCE
