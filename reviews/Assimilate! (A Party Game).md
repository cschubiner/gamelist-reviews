# Assimilate! (A Party Game)

## Game Information
- **Developer:** Friendship is Fun Studios
- **Publisher:** Friendship is Fun, LLC
- **Platforms:** Steam (Windows)
- **Release Date:** August 26, 2021
- **Price:** $9.99
- **Player Count:** 5-10 players (online or local)

## Overview
Assimilate! is a 50s-themed social deduction party game where a cleverly-disguised robot is trying to infiltrate a local, "humans only" sockhop. Players take on roles as either humans, the robot, or the programmer (the robot's best friend), working together within their teams to achieve victory.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
The game consists of three rounds of finger-pointing fun. Each round displays 20 passwords on the main TV screen, and humans and the programmer receive the same single password on their devices. Humans must describe their password with three clues without making it too obvious which password they're calling out. The robot, who doesn't know the password, must try to blend in by creating believable clues based on what they observe.

The core gameplay loop creates real incentives for humans to be subtle enough that they might accidentally trick their partners, adding tension and humor to each round. Players use their browsers on computers or mobile devices to participate.

## Fun Factor
There are moments of genuine fun in Assimilate!, particularly when players successfully deceive each other or when the robot manages to blend in convincingly. The 50s sockhop theme adds charm and personality to the experience. However, reviewers note that games typically last about 15 minutes, and players may want to move onto something else after about half an hour of play.

## Player Count Experience
Designed for 5-10 players, the game works best with groups in the middle of this range. Too few players and the game lacks complexity; too many and discussion phases can feel drawn out. The social deduction mechanics create engaging moments of suspicion and revelation among players.

### Key Takeaways
Reviewers found Assimilate! to be an enjoyable but limited experience. The core gameplay is fun with clever mechanics that encourage subtle communication, but the game suffers from pacing issues. Discussion sections run too long with timers that can't be accelerated, and games often continue even after the robot is discovered. Players wanted more customization options for passwords and the ability to end games early. At $9.99, it's a decent social deduction game for parties, though it may not have the longevity of other titles in the genre.
