# Sea Of Fatness: Save Humanity Together - Review Summary

Based on my research, I found limited professional reviews for "Sea Of Fatness: Save Humanity Together," as this is a small indie game released in 2017. However, I've compiled information from the available sources. Here's a structured summary:

---

## **SEA OF FATNESS: SAVE HUMANITY TOGETHER - REVIEW SUMMARY**

### **OVERALL RECEPTION**
- **Steam User Rating:** Mostly Positive (72% positive from 11 reviews)
- **Metacritic:** No professional critic reviews available
- **Release Date:** October 19, 2017
- **Developer:** Fusion.Robot & Co
- **Platforms:** PC, Mac, Linux

---

### **GAMEPLAY MECHANICS**

**Core Mechanics:**
- Simple click-based spawning system where players spawn burgers and apples to control humans
- Objective-based gameplay focused on sinking Mr. TrumPET's ark by causing collisions with sea mines
- Physics-based collision mechanics drive the primary gameplay loop
- Nearly 50 levels with varying difficulty

**Game Length:**
- Campaign: 1.5-3 hours depending on skill level

**Additional Modes:**
- "Beach Burger Balls" - A local versus mode described as volleyball-style gameplay with burgers and mines
- Steam Workshop support for custom level creation

---

### **PLAYER COUNT EXPERIENCE**

**Multiplayer Support:**
- **1-4+ players** (local multiplayer only)
- Unique multi-input system supporting:
  - Xbox controllers
  - Steam controllers
  - Multiple mice simultaneously (up to 10 mice with 'ExerWorldLinker' helper application on Windows)

**Reception on Multiplayer:**
- Mixed feedback: While the technical capability to support many mice is novel, reviewers noted it "doesn't make the game good"
- Limited fun factor in both single-player and local multiplayer modes according to critical users
- Best experienced as a social/party game rather than serious gameplay

---

### **FUN FACTOR**

**Positive Reception:**
- Praised for its absurdist humor and satirical premise
- One reviewer: "a game that i never knew i needed, but i'm so happy it exists"
- Appreciated for its psychedelic aesthetic and political satire
- Novel concept of "naked fat guys walking around" resonated with fans of absurd humor

**Critical Reception:**
- Some users found limited replayability
- Gameplay described as simple and straightforward, lacking depth
- Satirical theme may not appeal to all audiences

---

### **CRITICAL COMMENTARY**

**Le Temps (Swiss Publication):**
- Described the game as offering a "trash" aesthetic targeting contemporary US politics
- Developer Beat Kunz explained: "The world is so bizarre today. This is how I react as a game designer"

**Drop Rate:**
- Called it "an unexpectedly delightful experience"

**User Reviews:**
- "The kind of representation we deserve"
- "Excellent game" (from August 2018 curator review)
- Praised for being a satirical response to political absurdity

---

### **STRENGTHS**
- Unique satirical concept and political commentary
- Flexible multiplayer input options
- Absurdist humor appeals to niche audience
- Steam Workshop support extends replayability

### **WEAKNESSES**
- Limited professional critical coverage
- Simple mechanics may lack depth for some players
- Controversial political theme limits mainstream appeal
- Multiplayer features don't significantly enhance gameplay experience

---

### **CONCLUSION**

"Sea Of Fatness: Save Humanity Together" is a niche indie title that succeeds as a satirical art piece more than a traditional game. Its absurdist political commentary and unique aesthetic appeal to players looking for unconventional experiences, but its simple mechanics and limited scope prevent it from achieving broader acclaim. The game works best as a conversation piece or party game rather than a deep gameplay experience.
