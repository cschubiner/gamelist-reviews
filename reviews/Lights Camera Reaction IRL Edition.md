# Lights, Camera, Reaction! IRL Edition Review

## Overview
Lights, Camera, Reaction! IRL Edition is a couch-style multiplayer party game designed for PC that brings physical party game fun to the digital space. Supporting 3-8 players, it uses smartphones or tablets as controllers while players gather around a shared display for various mini-game competitions.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
Players create avatars by drawing a face, uploading an image, or taking a selfie, then connect to the party using their phones or tablets. The game features 6 selectable game modes that encourage different types of interaction:

- **Acting Out Words:** Players perform charades-style activities
- **Drawing Pictures:** Creative challenges requiring artistic interpretation
- **Jelly Blob Battles:** Physical competition elements
- **Blocking Mechanics:** Strategic gameplay where players interfere with opponents
- **Various Mini-Games:** Additional 2D and 3D competitive challenges

The setup requires a desktop PC or laptop connected to a TV, projector, or large display, along with comfortable seating and a medium to large room with space for physical activities.

## Fun Factor
The IRL Edition emphasizes getting players off the couch and physically active, blending digital gaming with real-world interaction. The variety of mini-games ensures different types of fun, from creative challenges to physical activities, keeping groups engaged through different interaction styles.

The ability to customize avatars adds personalization, and the mix of digital and physical activities creates a unique party experience that goes beyond traditional video games or board games.

## Player Count Experience
- **Players:** 3-8 players
- **Best For:** Groups of 4-8 players
- **Space Requirements:** Medium to large room with space for physical activities

The 3-8 player range is ideal for small to medium-sized gatherings, though the game likely shines with the full 8 players to maximize competition and energy.

## Verdict
