# Pixel Fishies

## Overview
**Platform:** PC (Steam)
**Players:** 1-16 players (local multiplayer on shared screen)
**Price:** Available on Steam
**Release Date:** Released on Steam
**Developer/Publisher:** Unknown

## Gameplay & Mechanics

Pixel Fishies is a retro-inspired game where 1-16 players lead schools of fish in a survival competition on a shared screen. The core gameplay revolves around gathering food, growing your school, and either competing against or cooperating with other players depending on the chosen mode.

Each player leads a school of fish with distinct personality traits that significantly impact gameplay strategy:

- **Blue Fish** - Superior hunters that seek food farther away than any other school, ideal for aggressive food gathering
- **Green Fish** - Fastest swimmers with the ability to swim at steep angles, offering mobility advantages
- **Yellow Fish** - Friendly and non-aggressive, never attack other schools and stay close to their leader (co-op friendly)
- **Red Fish** - Aggressive toward other schools, prioritizing attacking other fish over food gathering

The game supports flexible play styles: players can choose different colored schools to compete against each other in survival battles, or select the same school color for cooperative play. Up to 4 teams can form with up to 16 local players, creating chaotic battles for tiny plankton and dominance of the sea.

Victory conditions vary based on mode - multiplying your school, becoming the only surviving school, or defeating special enemies like squids.

## Fun Factor

Pixel Fishies offers a unique, quirky multiplayer experience centered around aquatic ecosystem competition. Players have highlighted moments like "killing and eating the squid" as particularly entertaining. The variety of fish personalities creates emergent gameplay where different strategies can succeed - aggressive red fish might dominate through combat while blue fish focus on outgrowing opponents through superior hunting.

The retro pixel art aesthetic and simple controls make it accessible for casual groups, though the shared-screen limitation and niche concept may not appeal to all audiences. The game works best when players embrace the chaotic, lighthearted nature of fish school warfare.

## Player Count Experience

**Optimal:** 4-8 players
**Supported:** 1-16 players (local only, up to 4 teams)
**Solo Play:** Technically supported but misses the competitive/cooperative core

With support for up to 16 local players divided into 4 teams, Pixel Fishies can accommodate large groups. However, with 16 players on a shared screen competing for visibility and space, the experience may become overwhelming. Mid-range player counts (4-8) likely offer the best balance between competitive dynamics and screen clarity.

The game is designed primarily for local multiplayer fun rather than solo play. Single-player mode exists but lacks the competitive energy that drives the core appeal.

## Technical Considerations

As a retro-inspired pixel art game, Pixel Fishies should have modest system requirements and run on most modern PCs without issue. The shared-screen design eliminates networking concerns but requires players to share one display, which may be challenging with higher player counts if the screen size is insufficient.

## Verdict

Pixel Fishies is a quirky, accessible party game that offers something different from typical competitive multiplayer experiences. The fish school competition mechanic, combined with distinct personality-based strategies, creates an unusual but potentially entertaining group gaming experience. It's best suited for casual groups looking for lighthearted, easy-to-learn multiplayer fun with a unique theme.

However, the niche concept and limited coverage make it a risky purchase. Those seeking deep strategic gameplay or polished production values should look elsewhere. Pixel Fishies works best as a novelty party game for groups willing to embrace its strange charm and simple mechanics.

**Recommended for:** Casual party gamers, groups seeking unique/quirky games, fans of simple competitive mechanics, retro game aesthetics enthusiasts

**Not recommended for:** Competitive gamers seeking depth, solo players, those requiring online multiplayer, groups without shared screen access
