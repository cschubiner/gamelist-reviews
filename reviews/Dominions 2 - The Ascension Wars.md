# Dominions 2 - The Ascension Wars

## Overview
Dominions II: The Ascension Wars is a turn-based fantasy strategy game where players take the role of a pretender god commanding one of seventeen distinct cultures. Released in 2003 as the sequel to the original Dominions, it expands on the series' deep strategic gameplay with more nations, refined mechanics, and complex systems.

## Gameplay & Mechanics

### Pretender God Customization
- Choose from seventeen cultures, each with unique strengths and weaknesses
- Customize your god with various attributes
- Options ranging from magic-focused but physically weak gods
- To near-unkillable warrior-gods with minimal magical powers
- Strategic pretender design crucial to success

### Scales System
Six scales affect your nation's characteristics:
1. Order/Turmoil
2. Productivity/Sloth
3. Heat/Cold
4. Growth/Death
5. Luck/Misfortune
6. Magic/Drain

### Army Command and Customization
- Flexible army composition options
- Multiple ways to command and customize forces
- Unit variety across different cultures
- Strategic troop deployment

### Magic and Research
- Complex magic system
- Multiple paths of magical research
- Spell research unlocks progressively powerful abilities
- Tactical and strategic spell usage

### Battle System
- Tactical combat resolution
- Large-scale battles
- Unit positioning and strategy
- Commander abilities affect outcomes

## Fun Factor
Dominions II offers incredibly deep strategic gameplay that can consume hundreds of hours. The game rewards careful planning, understanding of complex systems, and long-term strategic thinking. The learning curve is steep, requiring constant manual reference, but players who invest the time find an immensely rewarding experience with nearly endless strategic possibilities. Best for hardcore strategy enthusiasts willing to master complex systems.

## Notes
Dominions II represents a significant step forward from the original Dominions, expanding from fourteen to seventeen cultures and refining game systems. However, it maintains the series' reputation for complexity and steep learning curve. Players new to the series might find later entries (Dominions 5 or 6) more accessible while offering similar depth.

The game is particularly noted for its scale and ambition, attempting to create an epic fantasy strategy experience unlike anything else available at the time.
