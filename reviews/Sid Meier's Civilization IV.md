# Sid Meier's Civilization IV

## Overview
Sid Meier's Civilization IV is the fourth main installment in the groundbreaking Civilization series. Released in 2005, it brought significant innovations including a fully 3D engine, enhanced diplomacy, religion systems, and great people mechanics. Often considered one of the best entries in the franchise, Civilization IV represents a high point in the series' evolution.

## Gameplay & Mechanics

### Core 4X Gameplay
- **eXplore**: Discover the world, resources, and rival civilizations
- **eXpand**: Found cities and grow your empire
- **eXploit**: Manage resources and develop infrastructure
- **eXterminate**: Engage in warfare, diplomacy, or cultural expansion

### New Features (for the series at the time)
- **Religion System**: First introduction of religion mechanics
- **Great People**: Specialists can generate great people with powerful abilities
- **Civics**: Replace governments with more flexible civic choices
- **Vassalage**: Diplomatic option to create vassal states
- **Espionage**: More robust spy system

### 3D Engine
- Fully 3D graphics (first in the series)
- Improved visual presentation
- More detailed unit and building models
- Better terrain visualization

### Victory Conditions
- Domination (military conquest)
- Space Race (scientific victory)
- Cultural (three legendary cities)
- Diplomatic (United Nations vote)
- Conquest (eliminate all opponents)
- Time-based (highest score)

### Civilization Selection
- Multiple unique civilizations with distinct bonuses
- Famous historical leaders
- Unique units and buildings per civilization
- Different strategic approaches encouraged

## Fun Factor
Civilization IV perfected many aspects that made the series beloved. The "one more turn" addictiveness is at its peak, with the new religion and great people systems adding fresh strategic layers. The game strikes an excellent balance between complexity and accessibility. The expansions (Warlords and Beyond the Sword) further refined the experience and added significant content, making it a complete and deeply satisfying strategy game.

## Player Count Experience
- **Single Player**: Robust campaign against intelligent AI
- **Multiplayer**: Online and LAN support
  - Simultaneous or turn-based modes
  - Hot seat for local play
  - Play-by-email option
  - Up to 18 civilizations per game (with expansions)

## Platforms
- PC (Windows)
- Mac OS X
- Available on Steam and GOG

## Pricing
- Often available at budget prices
- Complete Edition includes all expansions
- "Unlike the newer instalments, Civilization IV is much less demanding in terms of hardware and finances but is just as addictive"
- Frequently on sale

## Overall Reception

### Critical Reception
Civilization IV is widely regarded as one of the best strategy games ever made.

**Universal Praise:**
- "Civilization IV brings back everything that makes this series great, and adds enough innovation to keep things fresh and approachable for both veteran and newcomer"
- "Civ will suffice, and it gets deeper each time you play"
- Considered by many to be the best Civilization game
- Exceptional balance of depth and accessibility

**Expansion Success:**
- Beyond the Sword expansion particularly praised
- Added substantial content and refinements
- Complete package considered near-perfect

### Player Feedback

**Strengths:**
- "Great starting point for getting to know the series"
- Less demanding on hardware than newer entries
- Just as addictive as modern installments
- Deep and replayable
- Excellent AI opponent behavior
- Strong modding community
- Religion system adds strategic depth
- Great people mechanics create memorable moments

**Why Players Love It:**
- Perfect balance of features
- Refined gameplay systems
- Memorable music and atmosphere
- Leonard Nimoy narration for technologies
- More streamlined than Civ III, less complex than later entries
- Expansions dramatically improve base game

**Longevity:**
- Still actively played today
- Active modding community
- Multiple total conversion mods available
- Considered by many superior to later entries

### Scores
- **Metacritic User Score**: 8.4 (Generally Favorable)
  - 839 positive reviews (82%)
  - 139 mixed reviews (14%)
  - Minimal negative reviews
- **Critical Reviews**: Universally acclaimed
- **Community**: Often cited as best in series

### Legacy
Civilization IV set a high bar that some argue hasn't been surpassed. Many hardcore Civilization fans consider it the peak of the series, with the perfect balance of complexity, accessibility, and features. It remains a benchmark for 4X strategy games.

## Notes
- Highly recommended to get the Complete Edition with Beyond the Sword expansion
- Beyond the Sword expansion considered essential
- Less demanding system requirements than later entries
- Famous Leonard Nimoy technology narration
- "Baba Yetu" opening theme (first video game music to win a Grammy)
- Still has active multiplayer community
- Extensive mod support including Fall from Heaven II (fantasy total conversion)

## Comparison to Later Games
Many players debate whether Civilization V or VI surpassed IV:
- **Civ IV fans cite**: Better AI, religion system, civic flexibility, great people
- **Civ V fans prefer**: No unit stacking, hexagonal grid, streamlined mechanics
- **Civ VI fans prefer**: District system, more depth, modern features
- All three are excellent; choice often comes down to personal preference
