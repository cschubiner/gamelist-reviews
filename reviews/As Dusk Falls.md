# As Dusk Falls Review

## Overview
As Dusk Falls is an interactive drama game from INTERIOR/NIGHT that blends cinematic storytelling with player choice mechanics. Released in July 2022, it tells an emotionally charged story spanning two families over thirty years, featuring a unique painted art style and robust multiplayer decision-making features.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
As Dusk Falls is a narrative-driven experience where players make choices that dramatically affect the story's direction. The game spans approximately seven hours for a single playthrough, with significant branching paths that encourage multiple playthroughs to explore different outcomes.

**Multiplayer Features:**
- **Up to 8 players** can participate in decision-making
- **Companion app** available for Android and iPhone
- Players use controllers or phones to vote on story decisions
- Most decisions are timed, adding pressure
- Jackbox-style multiplayer where the group collaboratively shapes the narrative

The game focuses on moral dilemmas, character relationships, and the consequences of split-second decisions, creating a shared storytelling experience.

## Fun Factor
As Dusk Falls creates engagement through its emotionally impactful narrative and meaningful player choices. The multiplayer aspect transforms it into a social experience where groups debate decisions and collectively deal with consequences. Critics praised:
- Strong writing and acting
- Genuine consequences for actions
- Emotional impact of choices
- Cooperative decision-making dynamics

However, the later chapters are noted to "falter somewhat in their effectiveness," and "the ending feels unnecessarily jarring," suggesting pacing issues in the final act.

## Player Count Experience
- **Players:** 1-8 players
- **Multiplayer Method:** Controllers and/or smartphone companion app
- **Best For:** Groups of 2-4 players, though supports up to 8
- **Playtime:** ~7 hours per playthrough

The companion app integration works smoothly, allowing easy drop-in multiplayer. The game is designed with multiplayer in mind, making group play the recommended experience rather than an afterthought.

## Overall Reception

### Critical Scores
- **OpenCritic:** Recommended by 71% of critics (Strong approval)
- **Critical Consensus:** Generally positive, with specific praise for narrative elements

### Critical Reception Highlights
**Praised Elements:**
- Storytelling and narrative quality
- Unique painted art style
- Voice acting and performances
- Emotional impact of choices
- Co-op/multiplayer mode implementation
- Meaningful consequences for player decisions

**Criticized Elements:**
- Later chapters falter in effectiveness
- Ending feels unnecessarily jarring
- Clunky controls
- Some pacing issues

### Strengths
- Exceptional storytelling with emotional depth
- Unique painted visual style
- Robust 8-player multiplayer support
- Seamless companion app integration
- Genuine consequences for choices
- High replay value through branching narratives
- Strong voice acting and writing
- ~7 hour playtime with substantial replayability
- Available on Xbox Game Pass
- Transforms narrative games into group experiences

### Weaknesses
- Later chapters are less effective
- Jarring ending
- Clunky control scheme
- Not as interactive as traditional games
- Story-focused, may not appeal to action gamers
- Ending quality doesn't match earlier chapters
- Limited "gameplay" in traditional sense

## Verdict
As Dusk Falls successfully brings cinematic interactive storytelling to the multiplayer space, creating a unique shared narrative experience. The 71% critical recommendation and praise for writing, acting, and meaningful choices demonstrate its quality as an interactive drama. The 8-player support with intuitive app integration makes it an excellent choice for groups who enjoy story-driven experiences.

The painted art style gives it a distinctive visual identity, and the emotional weight of decisions creates memorable group moments as players debate the "right" choice. While the ending disappoints and controls feel clunky, the overall package delivers a compelling 7-hour journey with substantial replay value.

Best suited for players who enjoy narrative-focused games like Detroit: Become Human or Telltale titles, particularly those wanting to experience such stories with friends. The Xbox Game Pass availability makes it a low-risk high-reward option for subscribers. Groups that appreciate moral dilemmas, character-driven stories, and collaborative decision-making will find As Dusk Falls a worthwhile experience, despite its narrative stumbles in the final act.
