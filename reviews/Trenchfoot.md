# Trenchfoot

## Overview
**Platform:** PC (Steam)
**Players:** Local multiplayer only, up to 16 players
**Price:** Available on Steam
**Release Date:** February 6, 2021 (Early Access: March 9, 2018)
**Developer/Publisher:** Cocompadres

## Gameplay & Mechanics

Trenchfoot is a fast-paced local multiplayer party game inspired by classic titles like Bomberman, designed specifically for up to 16 players on a single screen. The game eschews single-player content entirely, focusing exclusively on the chaotic, competitive multiplayer experience that made old-school party games so beloved.

The core mechanics revolve around strategic block placement and unique flood explosions. Players can place defensive blocks that stop explosions from spreading, allowing for dynamic battlefield shaping and tactical gameplay. The standout feature is the flood explosion system - these explosions don't follow traditional straight-line patterns but instead fill available space, flowing around corners, squeezing through bottlenecks, and expanding into open areas. This creates unpredictable and exciting chain reactions.

## Fun Factor

For fans of old-school multiplayer games like Bomberman, Trenchfoot delivers a familiar yet fresh experience. The flood explosion mechanic adds a layer of strategy and chaos that distinguishes it from its inspirations. The ability to support up to 16 simultaneous players on one screen creates hectic, laughter-filled sessions perfect for large gatherings.

However, the game's fun is entirely dependent on having other local players available - there is no single-player content or online multiplayer, which significantly limits its accessibility for solo gamers or those without regular access to large groups.

## Player Count Experience

**Optimal:** 4-8 players
**Supported:** 2-16 players (local only)
**Solo Play:** Not supported

The game scales from 2 players up to a chaotic 16-player experience. While the upper limit allows for massive party sessions, the sweet spot is likely in the 4-8 player range where strategy can still emerge from the chaos. With 16 players, the screen can become overwhelming, though this may be part of the intended fun for some groups.

The strict local multiplayer requirement means players need physical access to controllers and a shared screen, making this a couch party game in the traditional sense.

## Technical Considerations

As the game was in Early Access from 2018 before its 2021 full release, it has had time to mature. However, specific technical performance details are limited in available reviews. The game's requirement for local multiplayer only means no server infrastructure concerns, but players will need sufficient controllers for their desired player count.

## Verdict

Trenchfoot is a specialized title aimed squarely at groups who regularly gather for local multiplayer gaming sessions. If you're a fan of Bomberman-style games and have a consistent friend group available for couch gaming, Trenchfoot offers an engaging, modernized take on the formula with its unique flood explosion system. However, the complete absence of single-player or online modes makes this a niche purchase that will sit unplayed for those without regular local multiplayer opportunities.

**Recommended for:** Local multiplayer enthusiasts, fans of Bomberman, party game collectors, groups with 4+ regular players

**Not recommended for:** Solo players, online multiplayer seekers, casual gamers without regular gaming groups
