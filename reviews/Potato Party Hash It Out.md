# Potato Party: Hash It Out

## Game Information
- **Developer:** Alt Lab VR
- **Publisher:** Alt Lab VR
- **Platforms:** Meta Quest, Oculus Quest, Oculus Rift, Rift S, HTC Vive, Valve Index, Steam (VR)
- **Release Date:** 2021
- **Price:** $9.99
- **Player Count:** 1 VR player + 1-8 mobile challengers (2-9 total)

## Overview
Potato Party: Hash It Out is a quick-draw asymmetrical VR game where you (the VR potato) compete against your friends (iOS and Android potatoes) as they race to be the first to guess what you're drawing in three dimensions. One player acts as the artist in VR while 1-8 challengers guess using a free iOS/Android app.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
The game functions like 3D Pictionary, with the VR player drawing objects or concepts in three-dimensional space while mobile players watch and try to guess correctly. The asymmetrical gameplay creates an engaging dynamic where only one player needs VR equipment while others can participate freely with their smartphones.

The free companion mobile app makes the game highly accessible for multiplayer sessions, allowing up to 8 additional players to join without needing their own VR headsets. This design makes it an excellent showcase game for VR owners who want to include friends in the experience.

## Fun Factor
Players consistently report having "some of the most fun playing a mobile game in forever." The 3D drawing mechanic adds a unique twist to the classic guessing game format, and watching someone struggle to draw in 3D space often creates hilarious results. The game successfully bridges VR and non-VR players, making it ideal for social gatherings where only one person has a headset.

## Player Count Experience
The 1 VR + 1-8 mobile players format works exceptionally well for parties and gatherings. The VR player gets the immersive experience of creating 3D art, while mobile players enjoy watching and competing to guess first. This asymmetrical approach means VR owners can finally play with all their friends without everyone needing expensive equipment.

## Overall Reception

### Critic Scores

### User Scores
- **Steam:** 85% Positive (14 user reviews)

### Key Takeaways
Potato Party: Hash It Out successfully solves one of VR gaming's biggest challenges: social inclusion. By allowing one VR player to interact with up to 8 mobile players, it creates genuinely fun party experiences. Users praise it as "a pretty fun game for gatherings" and appreciate that "VR needs more games like this that you can play VR with your friends without needing everyone to have a headset."

However, there's a notable caveat: the Android app was delisted a few years ago, so Android users must either download an APK or the group needs to use iPhones. This limitation may affect accessibility for some groups. Despite this issue, the game remains a strong value at $9.99 for VR owners looking for party games.
