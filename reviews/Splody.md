# SPLODY - Critical Review Summary

## Overview
Splody is a multiplayer bomb-placement game developed by Dashing Strike that serves as a modern take on the classic Bomberman formula. The game supports 4-20 players in both local and online modes, with PC and PS4 releases.

---

## GAMEPLAY & MECHANICS

**Core Design:**
- Faithful recreation of Bomberman gameplay: place bombs, collect power-ups, eliminate opponents
- Controls described as feeling "exactly like Bomberman," making it immediately accessible
- Power-ups include bomb count increases, flame length extensions, kick abilities, and speed boosts
- Unique additions include dinosaur mounts (granting extra lives/hits) and dynamite (controlled explosions)

**Map Features:**
- Various environmental elements: conveyor belts, pipes, teleporters, darkness modes
- Debris-filled levels requiring strategic obstacle clearing
- Customizable map sizes and game modes (free-for-all, team mode, last man standing)

**Critical Consensus:**
Critics unanimously note the gameplay is "directly lifted" from Bomberman with "little to no changes beyond superficial power-ups or visual differences." Video Chums stated it "doesn't offer much substance that you can't already get from Hudson Soft's classic franchise."

---

## PLAYER COUNT EXPERIENCE

**Standout Feature:**
- **4-20 players locally** - A rare achievement that addresses the gap in couch competitive games supporting more than 4 players
- **Up to 64 players online** - Described as "chaotic" and entertaining to watch

**Major Issue:**
PlayStation Country reported a critical problem during their review: "there was not a single other player to find online," severely limiting the game's core multiplayer appeal. The game relies heavily on its multiplayer component, and empty servers significantly impact the experience.

**Bot Support:**
AI opponents are available as fill-ins, with reviewers noting "computers don't go down easy" at Normal difficulty and above.

---

## FUN FACTOR

**Positive Aspects:**
- **GameTyrant:** Described the experience as "addictive" with high replayability, comparing it favorably to "versing friends from my Bomberman 64 childhood days"
- **Thumb Culture:** Acknowledged it could "make for a competitive evening of explosive fun" when combined with "pizza, beer and a host of gaming friends"
- Social/party game potential with friends and family

**Negative Aspects:**
- **Solo play exhausts quickly** - Single-player against bots loses appeal "in less than an hour"
- **Short-lived enjoyment** - Initial fun diminishes without human opponents
- **Ghost bomber mechanic divisive** - Dead players can place bombs as phantoms, which is "funny initially but competitively annoying"
- Thumb Culture noted frustration with the "frantic pace"

---

## OVERALL RECEPTION

**Critical Scores:**
- **PlayStation Country:** 3/10
- **Video Chums:** 4/10
- **Thumb Culture:** Bronze Award (conditional recommendation)
- **GameTyrant:** Positive but notes missing campaign mode

**Steam User Reviews:** 92% positive (83 reviews)

**Professional Critics' Verdict:**

**Harshly Negative:**
- PlayStation Country called it "the most derivative clone of Bomberman to exist since the 90's and lacks any sense of uniqueness or personality of its own"
- Critics emphasized it "denotes a lack of passion within the manufacture and that Splody is merely a product of cynicism"
- "Cannot recommend it" when superior alternatives like Super Bomberman R exist

**Moderately Positive:**
- GameTyrant praised it as "a well-balanced, entertaining multiplayer game with addictive mechanics"
- Thumb Culture acknowledged it as family-friendly entertainment but "doesn't quite match classic Bomberman"

**Key Criticisms:**
1. Zero originality - formulaic Bomberman clone
2. No single-player campaign mode
3. Empty online lobbies (at time of reviews)
4. Lacks staying power for solo play
5. No unique identity or personality

**Key Strengths:**
1. Solid implementation of classic Bomberman mechanics
2. Exceptional local multiplayer support (4-20 players)
3. Extensive customization options
4. Lower price point than AAA alternatives
5. Strong party game potential with friends

---

## FINAL ASSESSMENT

Splody is a competent but uninspired Bomberman clone that succeeds primarily as a local multiplayer party game. While it offers impressive player counts and faithful mechanics, critics universally fault it for lacking originality and failing to justify its existence alongside established franchises. The game shines in social settings with multiple players but falls flat in solo play and suffers from sparse online populations. Best suited for groups seeking affordable couch competitive gameplay rather than those wanting innovation or solo content.
