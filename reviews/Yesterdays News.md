# Yesterday's News

## Game Overview
Yesterday's News is a 3-8 player party game where players craft hilarious headlines with friends. Players score points by composing the wittiest headlines or by supplying the perfect words for their friends' creations, creating a comedic word game experience.

## Platforms
- PC (Steam)

## Player Count
- 3-8 players
- Friends or audience can join for free (only one player needs to own the game)
- Cross-Platform Multiplayer supported

## Pricing
$11.99 USD on Steam

## Gameplay & Mechanics
Players work together and compete to create humorous news headlines by:
- Contributing words to other players' headlines
- Crafting complete headlines from provided words
- Voting on the funniest or most creative results

The game features:
- Online PvP
- LAN PvP
- Shared/Split Screen PvP
- Online Co-op
- LAN Co-op
- Shared/Split Screen Co-op
- Cross-Platform Multiplayer

Only one player needs to own the game for others to join, making it accessible for groups.

## Fun Factor
The headline creation mechanic taps into similar humor as games like Cards Against Humanity but with more creative freedom. Players must think like both headline writers and comedians, leading to absurd and hilarious combinations. The cross-platform and free-join features make it easy to start a session with friends.

## Player Count Experience
- **Optimal Range**: 5-8 players
- **Minimum**: 3 players
- **Maximum**: 8 players
More players mean more creative word contributions and more opportunities for unexpected comedic combinations.
