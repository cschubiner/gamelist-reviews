# Bomb Fight

## Overview
**Platform:** PC (Steam)
**Players:** Up to 12 players (local multiplayer)
**Price:** Available on Steam (often 80% discount)
**Release Date:** February 25, 2020
**Developer:** Guillaume Opoix
**Publisher:** Outcorp

## Gameplay & Mechanics

Bomb Fight is a local-multiplayer action party game inspired by classic 16-bit era games, particularly Bomberman. The game combines nostalgic gameplay elements with modern "game-changer power skills" to create chaotic multiplayer battles.

**Core Gameplay:**
Like its inspiration Bomberman, players navigate grid-based arenas placing bombs to destroy obstacles and eliminate opponents. The fundamental challenge involves strategic positioning, timing bomb detonations, and avoiding both your own explosives and enemy attacks.

**Power Skills:**
The game introduces six distinct elemental power-up abilities that significantly impact gameplay:
- **FIRE:** Traditional explosive damage
- **ICE:** Freezing effects on enemies or terrain
- **ELECTRIC:** Electrical damage with potential chain effects
- **WIND:** Movement or projectile manipulation
- **BLACK HOLE:** Gravity-based attacks pulling objects/players
- **EARTH:** Ground-based abilities or defensive options

These abilities add strategic variety and "game-changer" moments that distinguish Bomb Fight from pure Bomberman clones.

**Game Modes:**
Features 6 different game modes providing variety in objectives and win conditions beyond simple elimination battles.

**Controller Support:**
Remarkably flexible input options:
- Up to 12 controllers (standard setup)
- Up to 4 players on one keyboard
- 2 players can share a single controller
- Maximum configuration: 12 players using 1 keyboard (4 players) + 4 controllers (8 players via 2-per-controller)

This controller sharing flexibility is exceptional for party games, allowing massive player counts even with limited hardware.

## Fun Factor

For Bomberman fans, Bomb Fight delivers familiar strategic bomb placement gameplay with modern twists through elemental powers. The 16-bit aesthetic captures nostalgic appeal while the power skills prevent it from feeling like a mere clone.

Supporting up to 12 simultaneous players creates chaotic, exciting battles where the arena fills with bombs, explosions, and elemental effects. The various game modes provide replay variety beyond standard deathmatch battles.

## Verdict

Bomb Fight presents an appealing concept - Bomberman-inspired gameplay with elemental powers and impressive 12-player support. The controller sharing flexibility demonstrates thoughtful design, and the six game modes suggest attempt at replay variety.

However, the damning statistic is 4 total Steam reviews nearly 5 years after release. This isn't a new game struggling to find its audience - it's a 2020 release that has essentially failed to attract any player base whatsoever. Such minimal engagement typically indicates either severe quality problems, complete absence of marketing, or both.

Without community validation and with such minimal adoption, Bomb Fight cannot be recommended despite its promising feature set. Even at steep discounts (80% off), the lack of any meaningful player base raises serious red flags about game quality, polish, and execution.

For Bomberman fans, numerous other alternatives exist with proven communities and validated quality. The 4 review count after 5 years speaks volumes about the game's inability to attract or retain players.
