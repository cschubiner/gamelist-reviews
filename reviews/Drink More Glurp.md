# Drink More Glurp

## Game Information
- **Developer:** Coherent Labs / Ice Flake Studios
- **Publisher:** Yogscast Games
- **Platforms:** Steam, Nintendo Switch
- **Release Date:** August 6, 2020
- **Price:** $9.99 / £7.99 / €8.89
- **Player Count:** Up to 20 local players (Party Mode), 1 player (Challenge Mode)

## Overview
Drink More Glurp is described as "QWOP on steroids" - a wacky physics-based turn-based party athletics game set on a distant world where aliens have copied Earth's summer games and got everything slightly wrong. Having binge-watched broadcasts of Earth TV, the aliens are convinced that advertising is the most important part of any sporting event.

## Gameplay & Mechanics
The game is a mixture of QWOP and Ragdoll Runners with YouTube product placement parodies. Sponsors randomly take control of events and twist them to fit their agenda, leading to a chaotic galactic summer games experience. Players compete in various athletic events using intentionally awkward physics-based controls.

The game supports up to 20 local players competing in crazy contests (Party Mode) or challenging the world on global leaderboards (Challenge Mode). The controls around jumping and aiming are intentionally chaotic, making even simple tasks hilariously difficult.

## Fun Factor
Drink More Glurp is ten times better with friends than alone. The intentional difficulty and chaotic physics create hilarious spectator moments, with lots of shouting and cheering expected. The game is best enjoyed in short bursts, with half-hour sessions being ideal. While the game is hard by design, that's precisely what makes it fun - watching players struggle with absurd physics challenges.

## Player Count Experience
Although it supports up to 20 players, the game is better enjoyed in smaller numbers (4-8 players) to keep game times short. In Party Mode, players compete for medals rather than just high scores, which adds a competitive edge. The turn-based format ensures everyone gets their moment in the spotlight (or embarrassment). Some reviewers noted that split-screen multiplayer could have enhanced the experience for simultaneous play.

### Key Takeaways
Drink More Glurp receives strong praise for its party game potential and value proposition. At its price point, reviewers consider it an "absolute bargain" that will give players, family, and friends "incredible laughs." The game excels as a spectator experience, making it perfect for parties and gatherings.

The main criticisms focus on the intentionally chaotic controls sometimes being "just the wrong side of chaotic" and the lack of split-screen multiplayer. However, most reviewers see the challenging controls as a feature rather than a bug, as they create the game's signature comedy. The game is best suited for groups who enjoy physics-based party games and aren't afraid of difficult, intentionally frustrating challenges. Solo play is significantly less engaging than multiplayer sessions.
