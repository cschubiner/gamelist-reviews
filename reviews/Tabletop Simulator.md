# Tabletop Simulator

## Overview
Tabletop Simulator is a comprehensive digital platform that allows players to play tabletop games virtually. With extensive modding support and the ability to load custom models and pieces, it offers near-limitless options for playing board games, card games, RPGs, and more with friends online.

## Gameplay & Mechanics

### Core Features
- Physics-based tabletop simulation
- Realistic piece manipulation
- Virtual game table with customizable environments
- Voice chat and text communication
- Save and load game states

### Base Game Content
- 15 classic games included:
  - Chess
  - Poker
  - Jigsaw Puzzles
  - Dominoes
  - Mahjong
  - And more traditional games

### Modding and Customization
- Built with modders in mind
- Load custom models and pieces
- Import custom textures and assets
- Create custom game boards
- Near-limitless customization options
- Extensive Steam Workshop integration
- Ability to create custom tabletop terrain

### Multiplayer
- Up to 10 players per session
- Online multiplayer
- Voice and text chat
- Spectator mode available
- Save states for continuing games later

## Fun Factor
Tabletop Simulator serves as a virtual game table, enabling people to play board games remotely with friends. The experience closely mimics physical tabletop gaming while adding conveniences like automatic dice rolling and piece manipulation. The extensive Workshop support means you can play almost any tabletop game imaginable. However, the experience is best viewed as a means to play games rather than an entertainment product in itself.

## Notes
Tabletop Simulator is essentially a tool that enables digital tabletop gaming. While it may not be "fun" in itself (compared to a traditional video game), its value lies in enabling people to play their favorite tabletop games remotely. Best suited for:
- Gaming groups separated by distance
- Trying games before purchasing physical copies
- Playing expensive or hard-to-find games
- Solo playtesting custom games

Be aware that some Workshop content may be removed due to DMCA claims, and popular games often receive official DLC releases that must be purchased separately.
